<h2><strong>Understanding Nic Salt Compatibility</strong></h2>
<h3><strong>What Makes a Pod Kit Nic Salt-Friendly?</strong></h3>
<p>Not every pod kit plays nice with salt-based liquids. Here's what separates the good from the garbage:</p>
<p><strong>Resistance matters more than you'd think.</strong> Nic salts shine in devices with higher resistance coils, typically between 0.8&Omega; and 1.5&Omega;. These coils run at lower wattages, which means:</p>
<ul>
<li>Less heat production</li>
<li>Smoother throat hit</li>
<li>Better flavour delivery</li>
<li>Longer coil lifespan</li>
</ul>
<p>Going below 0.8&Omega;? You're entering sub-ohm territory, which cranks up the power. That might work for freebase liquids, but it'll turn your <a href="https://simbavapes.co.uk/collections/elux-nic-salts"><strong>Elux Nic Salts</strong></a> experience into a harsh, overwhelming mess.</p>

<h2><strong>Common Mistakes to Avoid</strong></h2>
<h3><strong>The Wrong Resistance Trap</strong></h3>
<p>I've said it before, but it bears repeating: sub-ohm coils and nic salts don't mix well.</p>
<p>You might think lower resistance equals better flavour or bigger clouds. With nic salts, you're just setting yourself up for harsh hits and wasted e-liquid.</p>
<p>Stick to MTL-appropriate resistances. Your throat will thank you.</p>
<h3><strong>Overfilling Pods</strong></h3>
<p>That extra millilitre seems tempting. Don't do it.</p>
<p>Overfilled pods leak. They gurgle. They flood. Max fill lines exist for a reason &ndash; there needs to be space for air pressure equalisation.</p>
<p>Fill to the line, close it properly, wait a minute for the coil to saturate, then vape. Simple.</p>
<h3><strong>Ignoring Priming</strong></h3>
<p>Brand new coil? Don't just slap it in and fire away.</p>
<p>Prime it first:</p>
<ol>
<li>Install the new coil</li>
<li>Fill the pod</li>
<li>Wait 5-10 minutes</li>
<li>Take a few dry pulls (without firing)</li>
<li>Start at lower power, then increase</li>
</ol>
<p>This lets the cotton wick fully saturate. Skip this step, and you'll burn the coil before you've even started properly using it.</p>

<h2><strong>Frequently Asked Questions</strong></h2>
<p><strong>Can I use elux nic salts in any pod kit?</strong></p>
<p>Technically yes, but you shouldn't. Nic salts work best in MTL devices with resistance above 0.8&Omega;. Using them in sub-ohm setups designed for freebase liquids creates an overly intense, harsh experience. Match your liquid to the appropriate device type for the best results.</p>
<p><strong>How long do pod kit coils last with nic salts?</strong></p>
<p>Typically 1-2 weeks with moderate use. Factors affecting lifespan include vaping frequency, liquid sweetness, and how well you maintain the device. Sweeter liquids gunk up coils faster. If you're chain-vaping or using particularly sweet <a href="https://simbavapes.co.uk/collections/elux-nic-salts"><strong>Elux Legends Nic Salts</strong></a>, expect to replace coils more frequently.</p>
<p><strong>What's better for nic salts: prefilled or refillable pods?</strong></p>
<p>Refillable pods offer better value and flexibility. You can use any compatible nic salt, including your preferred elux legend nic salts, and switch flavours whenever you fancy. Prefilled pods are more convenient but limit your choices and cost more long-term. Choose based on whether you value convenience or cost-effectiveness.</p>
<p><strong>Do I need adjustable wattage for nic salt vaping?</strong></p>
<p>Not really. Most MTL pod kits have fixed wattage optimised for nic salts. Adjustable wattage offers fine-tuning options, but it's not essential. If your device runs between 10-15 watts, you're sorted. Adjustability becomes more useful if you occasionally vape freebase liquids too.</p>
<p><strong>How do I know if my pod kit is leaking?</strong></p>
<p>Check for e-liquid around the airflow slots, on the device body, or pooling in the pod. Slight condensation is normal, but actual liquid leaking isn't. Causes include overfilling, damaged seals, incorrect coil installation, or temperature changes. Fix the underlying issue rather than constantly wiping up leaks.</p>
<p><strong>Can I switch between different nic salt flavours in the same pod?</strong></p>
<p>Yes, but expect some flavour mixing initially. The old flavour lingers in the cotton until the new liquid flushes it out. For drastically different flavours (like switching from menthol to custard), you might want a fresh coil. For similar profiles, just vape through the transition period.</p>
<p><strong>What PG/VG ratio works best with elux nic salts?</strong></p>
<p>Most nic salts, including elux varieties, use 50/50 PG/VG ratios. This balanced mix works perfectly in MTL devices, providing good flavour, adequate throat hit, and appropriate vapour production. Higher VG ratios are too thick for many MTL coils, while higher PG can be too harsh.</p>
